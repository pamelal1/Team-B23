Australian Fur Seal




The Australian Fur Seal, Arctocephalus pusillus doriferus is the largest fur seal and a rare one. It is mostly found in Southern Victoria, parts of Tasmania. The adult seals are 2.5 metres in length. The colour of the seals is dark grey to brown. They have flippers. Their diet is mostly fish, squid, octopus.

They are threatened because of killer whales, sharks, fishing, swallowing plastic bags, entanglement with fishing nets.

Australian Sea Lion




The Australian Sea Lion, Neophoca cinerea is one of the most endangered species in the world. It is mostly found in Western Australia and Southern Australia, Victoria. Adults are 2-2.5 metres in length. They haev silver coat on theri backs and creamy yellow underneath. Their diet is cephalopods, crustaceans and fish.

Their threats are sharks, trash in the oceans.

Sub-Antarctic Fur Seal




The Sub-Antarctic Fur Seal, Artocephalus tropicalis is a small, carnivorous animal. Mostly found in southern Australia. The adult seals are 2 metres in length. The colour of the seals is chocolate brown with yellow face. Their diet is Myctophid fish and squid.

They are threatened due to fishing practices, oil spills.

New Zealand Fur Seal




The New Zealand Fur Seal, Arctocephalus forsteri is slightly smaller than the Australian Fur Seal with the aults growing upto 2.3 metres in length. They can be found in the southern aprt of Australia. They have a darker colour than the Australian Fur Seal. Their diet is Redbait and Jack Mackerel and myctophid species.

Their threats are human activities. They are listed under the threatened list due to their low numbers.
