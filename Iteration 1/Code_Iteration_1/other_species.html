Other Species

Although seals are important in Victoria, there are other species are prevalent in Victoria.

Other Species


Humpback Whales are graceful and magnificent. Their diet consists mostly of krill and small fish. These marine mammals travel great distances to take advantage of the best breeding grounds and feeding spots. There are two populations of humpback whales that migrate from Antarctica to Australia, one population migrates along the east coast of Australia and the other migrates along the coast of Western Australia. Some Humpback Whales enter Victorian waters on their migratory path and occasionally enter Port Phillip Bay and Western Port Bay.


The Bottlenose Dolphin can be found around the coast, sometimes they catch waves with surfers. Fish is one of the main items in the dolphin diet. They also eat shrimps, squid, mollusks, and cuttlefish, and only swallow the soft parts. Grey-headed Albatross have a 2.2 meters wingspan, a white body, a bluish grey head and neck, and a dark grey back and tail. Grey-headed albatross feed while on the sea surface. They eat fish and squid and to a lesser extent, crustaceans. Grey-headed albatross breed on many sub-antarctic islands. However, they tend to remain in sub-antarctic waters for rest time of the year. That is why they can be found around Victorian costal line.
